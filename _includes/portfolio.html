Application of optical flow algorithms to laser speckle imaging

Since of its introduction in 1980s, laser speckle imaging has become a powerful tool in flow imaging. Its high performance and low cost made it one of the preferable imaging methods. Initially, speckle contrast measurements were the main algorithm for analyzing laser speckle images in biological flows. Speckle contrast measurements, also referred as Laser Speckle Contrast Imaging (LSCI), use statistical properties of speckle patterns to create mapped image of the blood vessels. In this communication, a new method named Laser Speckle Optical Flow Imaging (LSOFI) is introduced. This method uses the optical flow algorithms to calculate the apparent motion of laser speckle patterns. The differences in the apparent motion of speckle patterns are used to identify the blood vessels from surrounding tissue. LSOFI has better spatial and temporal resolution compared to LSCI. This higher spatial resolution enables LSOFI to be used for autonomous blood vessels detection. Furthermore, Graphics Processing Unit (GPU) based LSOFI can be used for quasi real time imaging.

Convective Plume Visualization of Propagating Fire in Vegetative Fuel Bed Using Background Oriented Schlieren (BOS)

Heat transfer is very important in wildland fire spread. Recent findings have shown that fire will not spread without the presence of convective heat transfer. Unlike radiation, there is no direct method of measuring convection. Since convective heat transfer is governed by the fluid flow, understanding the fluid flow provides good understanding on the convective heat transfer. In fluid mechanics, flow visualization is a common methodology to understand the flow characteristics in various condition. Schlieren photography is a common flow visualization technique which captures changes in density such as the ones around a fire. Background-Oriented Schlieren (BOS) is a flow visualization technique which uses background with various patterns to visualize the density gradient caused by density fluctuations in the fluid. The initial result of BOS makes it possible to visualize the flow around the flame. Afterwards, the visualized flow can be processed to obtain the velocity structure of the flow. In this paper, BOS technique is used to visualize and characterize turbulent properties of a fire as it propagates in a solid vegetative fuelbed. The wind pushes the thermal plume ahead of the flame front, this is in contrast with the condition that no wind is present, in which thermal plume is attached to the fire. At last, a model for calculation of convective heat transfer from BOS data is introduced as well. This calculation shows that the presence of wind enhances convective heat transfer..
